FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What the heck is this?
Gowalla is a worldwide augmented reality social game that inspires you to explore. By joining the waitlist today you’ll be among the first to have access when we launch.
For real tho?
Oh yeah. Everyone enjoys a little nostalgia, right? Hard to believe that was a decade ago. If you played Gowalla back when, this will seem a little familiar—it ain’t about checkins though. We’re creating an entirely new experience along with one of the most advanced technology platforms from the ground up. We simply could not have built this in ye olde social mobile days.
Aaand when does it launch?
Gowalla beta for iOS will launch for a limited number of cities and members in summer 2021. Gowalla will launch for the public later next year. Want the earliest access and an opportunity to help curate the AR experience in your city? Join the Gowalla Street Team. Street Team app and Discord invites will be sent in December 2020.
But what about the…
Ugh, such a bummer. 2020 has been a hot mess in every way. Yet we’re so excited for people to re-explore their cities, support local businesses, and honestly just leave their homes in general. That’s why we're aiming to launch Gowalla beta next year, in summer 2021. If you want to help kickstart Gowalla in your city today (while you self-distance), consider joining the Street Team.
Who’s building this?
Gowalla is made by Josh Williams and Patrick Piemonte. And ultimately you. If you’d like to learn how you can help in your city, join the waitlist and let us know which community you’d like to be a part of.
Are you hiring?
You bet we are. We’re looking for a few stellar engineers, computer vision and machine learning experts, designers, 3D artists, and community support leads to join our founding team. Full time and contract opportunities are available. We’ll post more details soon. Until then, hit us up at jobs@gowalla.com. We are a remote-native company.
Anything else?
Yeah, Gowalla is incubated by Form Capital, a seed-stage fund founded by Bobby Goodlatte and Josh Williams. If you're building something epic that imagines a bright future for humanity, Form would like to hear from you.
